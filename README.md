# Youth Aging Out of Foster Care and the Risk of Homelessness: A Data Analysis

## Project Overview
Youth transitioning out of the foster care system face significant challenges when entering adulthood. Many young adults aging out of foster care lack financial stability, family support, and long-term housing options. This project explores whether youth who exit the foster care system are at a greater risk of experiencing homelessness and examines factors that may influence housing stability.

The goal of this analysis is to identify patterns between foster care transitions, support services, employment outcomes, education levels, and youth homelessness. 

---

## Research Question
What percentage of youth experiencing homelessness previously spent time in the foster care system?

### Secondary Questions
- Do support services for youth aging out of foster care reduce homelessness risk?
- How do employment and education outcomes influence housing stability after leaving foster care?

---

## Variables Analyzed

- Youth homelessness rate
- Housing stability available after aging out
- Employment rates
- Educational attainment
- Access to mental health services

## Analysis Plan

### 1. Foster Care Exits vs Youth Homelessness
Examines whether states with higher numbers of youth aging out of foster care also show higher youth homelessness rates.

### 2. Support Services and Housing Outcomes
Evaluates whether youth with access to transition programs experience greater housing stability.

### 3. Employment and Education Outcomes
Analyzes whether employment and educational attainment correlate with improved housing outcomes.
